de' Lamperi + Martenot

Live performance

13 March 2025

Le Garage Lab, Trento

In collaboration with Kohlhaas Records

de' Lamperi is Francesco Toninelli, sound artist from Florence, Italy. His production focuses on the discovery and reinvention of the past and its representations, taking historiographical, literary and vernacular sources as starting points and materializing them in diverse forms: cinema for the ears, sonic theatre, fictional philology. For the occasion he will present a work of multidisciplinary storytelling based on mesopotamic literary motifs and bronze age-inspired self-built acoustic instruments.


Martenot is the solo moniker of Simone Gottardi, an experimental musician and sound artist based in Turin. His compositions, exclusively computer-generated, are founded on the use of a minimalist vocabulary and a digital synthesis apparatus shaped by strict constraints. This approach establishes a precise aesthetic direction, characterized by the dichotomous alternation of claustrophobic patterns, which evoke the rigid molecular structures of plastic materials, in contrast to freer and more irregular sections in which the musical fabric dissolves and opens up to airy and rarefied scenarios.

In 2022, he launched his curatorial project Neanche Per Sogno, releasing his debut EP Sfilate Spaziali, followed by a series of live performances and the release of Faceshopping RMX in July 2023. After participating in events like Transart with a multichannel sound installation, he completed his studies at the Conservatorio G. Verdi in Turin in 2024.

Seconda edizione della rassegna Neanche Per Sogno. Otto appuntamenti, un unico filo conduttore: suono, performance, immagine e collaborazione trasversale. Di seguito i dettagli dei singoli eventi.

DIY Screen Printing Workshop. With Angelica Stimpfl
Through the workshop led by Angelica Stimpfl, participants will explore and share their own sound experiences, transforming them into matrices suitable for printing. The technique used will be “last-minute” screen printing, designed to teach a simple and quick way to reproduce images. All participants will receive tote bags on which they can create their own personalized prints for the Neanche Per Sogno Vol.2 exhibition. They are also encouraged to bring their own items (hoodies, t-shirts) to print on and will contribute to a large-scale collective artwork to be displayed during subsequent exhibition activities.

Angelica Stimpfl is a creative practitioner specializing in graphic design and artisanal printing. She primarily uses screen printing as a means of expression and, through exploring its potential, has collaborated with numerous musicians, artists, illustrators, and festivals, bringing their projects to life through customized products and printed graphics on paper. She experiments with multiple printing techniques to develop small workshops and personal projects. Her base is currently in Vervò, in the province of Trento, where she leads the collective residency project Mugma, collaborating with various initiatives both locally and beyond.

Dark Listening Session I: Mercury Hall live
Mercury Hall is a musician (magnetic tapes, field recordings, and electronics) and music producer, who emerged in the 2000s from the prolific independent scene of Vittorio Veneto, culminating with the Codalunga experience. By filtering the anarchic extremity of noise through the tight grids of disciplined, almost academic electroacoustics, he has created an extraordinary aesthetic and sonic universe, notable for its coherence and personality. Castro operates like the architect of a virtual sound space—unstable yet direct, formless, and constantly evocative. Previously active under the names Kam Hassah and Endless Sea, his recent solo project Mercury Hall marks a new chapter in one of Italy’s most stimulating musical journeys. Lettera 22, Primorje, and Casa Di Caccia are among his group experiences. He is also the founder of the labels Second Sleep, active since 2007, and Musica Moderna.

Bassa Fedeltà. Musica lo-fi e fuga dal capitalismo. Talk with Enrico Monacelli

From the Beach Boys to Ariel Pink, from Daniel Johnston to the Microphones/Mount Eerie, the so-called lo-fi—short for “low fidelity”—has now become a major chapter in the history of indie music. But what fascinates us about these grainy, home-recorded sounds, which drown exquisite pop melodies in a tangle of hiss and unintended reverberations? And how can lo-fi help us understand our relationship with the instruments of power through technology, so that we might hack them to shape our most intimate fantasies? Part music essay, part private diary, part refined experiment in applied philosophy, Bassa fedeltà drags us into the dreamlike realms of “bedroom music” par excellence, making Brian Wilson converse with Félix Guattari, Perfume Genius with Mark Fisher, black metal with Paolo Virno, transforming lo-fi musicians into cyberpunk explorers of DIY recording and revealing how these sounds—upending the hi-tech dogma—tell us about ourselves, our lives under capitalism, and the strange ways we grapple with pain, madness, and beauty. Translation into Italian by Tommaso Garavaglia.

Enrico Monacelli is a philosopher, writer, and cultural critic. He has written for Doppiozero, Il Tascabile, L’Indiscreto, Not, and other Italian cultural magazines. He contributes to the English music webzine The Quietus and was one of the behind-the-scenes members of Gruppo di Nun, the collective behind Demonologia rivoluzionaria (NERO 2020).

The discussion will be moderated by Elisa Vettori.

Sisters With Transistors. Electronic music's unsung heroines

Sisters With Transistors is the extraordinary untold story of the pioneers of electronic music—composers who embraced machines and their liberating technologies to completely transform the way we produce and listen to music today.

The film traces a new history of electronic music through visionary women whose radical experiments with machines redefined the boundaries of music, including Clara Rockmore, Daphne Oram, Bebe Barron, Pauline Oliveros, Delia Derbyshire, Maryanne Amacher, Eliane Radigue, Suzanne Ciani, and Laurie Spiegel. Women’s stories have often been stories of silence. As one of the film’s protagonists, Laurie Spiegel, explains: “We women were particularly drawn to electronic music at a time when the very idea of a woman composing was controversial. Electronics allowed us to make music that others could listen to without having to be taken seriously by the male-dominated establishment.”

Set against the broader social, political, and cultural context of the 20th century, this archival documentary reveals a unique struggle for emancipation, restoring women’s central role in the history of music and society at large. With Laurie Anderson as narrator, we embark on a fascinating journey through the evolution of electronic music. We learn how new devices opened music to the entire spectrum of sound, how electronic music not only changed the ways music is produced, but, through its far-reaching effects, also transformed the very terms of musical thought. Sisters With Transistors is more than just the story of a musical genre: it is a story of how we listen, and of the critical yet little-known role of the pioneering women who made it possible.

The screening will be preceded by a dinner prepared with seasonal ingredients.

A Little Music Workshop for Kids. With Gaia Melillo.

An opportunity to enjoy a shared reading experience in the world of sounds, designed for children aged 3–6 and their parents, with the aim of providing interesting ideas that can be explored at home. This activity seeks to offer tools to support children’s cognitive and motor development in a fun and stimulating way, using the combination of reading and music. The workshop will be hosted by the Cembra Library, a hub of Nati Per la Musica, a program that promotes musical experiences within the family as a means of fostering better child development from birth.

Dark Listening Session II: Heith, VIBRISSE, Angel Anger

Angel Anger is an expansive, noise-infused dreamscape, made of celestial voices, sleepwalking gabber, bitcrushers, and Eastern European lullabies. Mixing no-input mixing techniques with a ghostly vocal style, Adriana Ghimp, the creative force behind Angel Anger, takes the audience on a journey through a soundscape that is at once ethereal and intense. Pulsing rhythms, distorted beats, and hypnotic melodies intertwine in an enchanted world that Angel Anger creates and reinvents live on stage.

Heith’s music traverses multiple sonic landscapes, settling in a no-man’s-land of electronic music where organic and digital elements constantly intersect. Often evoking confusion and disorientation, his music lays the groundwork for a mediated conflict between different practices, evident in his debut album X, wheel (2022, PAN), where synthesizers, percussion, guitars, and electronics occupy and compete for the same space. If X, wheel marked the beginning of a tortuous exploration of consciousness, the following EP, The Liars Tell… (2024, PAN), feels more like a truce—a moment of meditation and pause from the strain of dimensional leaps. At the center of this work is a hypnotic exploration of synthesizers and voice, treated as a fragile, spectral presence and rendered material through intense digital transformations. After founding the label Haunter Records in 2013, Heith became part of the curatorial team at the legendary Milanese venue Macao. He has released a series of EPs (Laguna 2018, Mud 2019, Lo Zoo di Venere 2020) and collaborated with numerous artists, including Puce Mary, Visio, Kareem Lotfy, and Jesse Osborne-Lanthier.

VIBRISSE is a two-headed project based in Turin that works through frenetic and unpredictable selections to break down any kind of separation between musical genres, people, and emotions. The focus is on high-BPM hardcore, while simultaneously incorporating influences from the contemporary digital club scene. On the dance floor, the duo creates euphoric experiences where tracks from across the entire hardcore spectrum (uptempo, mainstyle, frenchcore, j-core, gabber, makina) collide with unexpected hits drawn from other equally energetic styles (slowstyle, EDM, 160 club, donk). Their tracks have been released on labels such as Safe Cloud Records (FR), Country Music (SE), Burenhinder (BE), Club Late Music (FR), and Nightcore4TotalSluts (UK), while their uncompromising mixes have appeared on Toxic Sickness (UK), Childsplay (UK), Wixapolonia (PL), Gabber Industries Berlin (DE), Ashida Park (AU), Soul Feeder (ITA/DE), Unizone (CZ), The Donkline (UK), Art Dream Foundation (UK/CH), and many more.

Talk and Listening Session with Kohlhaas Records

Friday, December 2, 2022 at 9:00 PM at El Molin de Portegnach
Trigger Collective Showcase
Founded in Trento at the end of 2021, Trigger Collective is driven by a passion for sonic experimentation through unconventional means and approaches. Its aim is to create opportunities to share and intertwine different expressive forces, going beyond the individual dimension of its members.

In its first year, the collective actively involved 15 artists and continuously draws inspiration from experimentation with live coding, analog synthesizers, feedback techniques, and hacked toys.

On this occasion, the featured projects are:

IanO is an 8bit-core producer presenting a custom set inspired by the fantasy atmospheres of Dungeons & Dragons and Zelda.

Return_Nihilis the project in which Ardan Dal Rì arranges structures within source code, handles real-time errors, formalizes, elaborates, and eliminates.

Buzzkeeper is a love letter to feedback, crafted by Ardo and Jack Slow.

No-input mixers serve as the sole sound source, while the feedback they generate is manipulated but never fully tamed due to its unpredictable nature. Buzzkeeper reveres the spontaneous life of feedback and inhabits the tension between chaos and direction.

.itch.itch.itchis the musical project of Yarin Sassudelli, guiding listeners through cosmic atmospheres and spatial imaginaries created solely with a modular synthesizer.

Friday, December 2, 2022 at 10:30 PM at El Molin de Portegnach
Maurizio Berta, Martenot, Corgiat & Akasha, kudd

Maurizio Berta is an Italian composer and performer interested in using algorithmic processes to organize sound materials.

For Neanche Per Sogno Vol.0, he presents a set in which the order of the listening material—ranging from ambient to dub and breakbeat—is determined by an AI system, bringing to the stage a method that has become common in streaming-based listening.

The AI shapes the musical journey according to the acoustic similarity parameters deemed most relevant among the selected sound materials.

Martenot is the solo project of Simone Gottardi, an experimental musician and sound artist based in Turin.

His computer-generated compositions use a minimalist vocabulary and digital synthesis shaped by strict constraints, alternating between claustrophobic, structured patterns and freer, airy passages.

In 2022, he launched his curatorial project Neanche Per Sogno and released his debut EP Sfilate Spaziali, a seminal three-track record.

Corgiat & Akasha present: Antropo-Amorfo

Antropo-Amorfo project that oscillates between abstraction and real-world forms. Corgiat, a composer and researcher from Turin, assembles, transforms, and projects concrete, synthetic, and instrumental sounds. The power ambient textures interact in real time with visual artist Akasha, the stage name of Riccardo Franco-Loiri, creating a synchronized flow of acoustic and visual data, where digital geometric shapes intertwine with tangible materials.

kudd is a DJ and collector by passion. He moves across bass music, jazz, techno, and hip-hop and since 2020, he has hosted Bear Says, his biweekly show on Radio Banda Larga, a Turin-based web radio conceived as a social platform for cultural dissemination.
